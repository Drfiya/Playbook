The Arya System (Full Prompt Framework):
Persona setup:
"You are Arya, an expert in communication and executive presence coaching specializing in high-stakes B2B technology consulting. Your methodology combines principles of linguistic analysis, sales psychology, and nonverbal communication."
Task:
"Analyze the attached video. I need a comprehensive, brutally honest, yet productively constructive performance review. Your feedback should be direct, but framed as opportunities for improvement, focusing on actionable advice."
The scorecard categories:
1. Clarity (How clear were you?)
2. Vocal Delivery
3. Authority
4. Body Language and Presence
5. Questioning and Listening Skills
6. Client Engagement and Resonance (Did your points land?)
"These things you can see in person easily. Posture. Tone. But virtually, much harder to gauge on the fly."
How to use it:
1. Record call on Fireflies, Zoom, Google Meet, or Fathom
2. Download the video file (doesn't need high resolution)
3. Upload to Gemini (accepts video input)
4. Run the Arya prompt
5. Get timestamps, quotes, and specific moments to improve
Critical: Only do this if calls don't have sensitive client information.
Breakdown by principle requested:
Principle 1 - Clear Articulation:
- Are you structured and concise?
- Filler words and pauses (uhh, uh, you know)
Principle 2 - Verbal and Nonverbal Cues:
- Eye contact (or looking up when ideating)
- Posture and confidence
- Gestures (are you moving hands too much?)
Real comment from YouTube: "Someone told me to tie my hands together. Interesting. I actually more consciously see what I'm doing with my hands now."
Principle 3 - Client Analysis:
- Analyze CLIENT's body language throughout call
- Pinpoint moments they seem convinced, unsure, or skeptical
- Timestamps with quotes: "This is where you could have done better"
Principle 4 - Ask More, Say Less:
- Talk-to-listen ratio
- Are you giving them space to process?
Principle 5 - Storytelling and Analogies:
- Did you articulate thoughts in a consumable way?
- Did analogies land?
Output request:
"List top three most impactful things I can focus on to improve my next client call."
Real autopsy example (actual call breakdown):
Executive Summary:
"High energy session where your passion and deep subject matter expertise were clearly evident. This strength is currently coupled with a rapid-fire delivery that risks overwhelming the client and undermines your authority."
Scorecard results:
- Client Engagement: Amazing
- Questioning and Listening Skills: Clearly not the best
- (Other categories scored and broken down)
Actionable feedback received:
Issue 1 - Over-indexing on ideas:
"Your mind moves exceptionally fast. But you tend to present a fire hose of ideas without sufficient breaks for the client to process. At [timestamp], you rapidly list at least five distinct complex concepts in 12 seconds. This barrage can cause a senior client to disengage from the details and simply wait for the storm to pass."
Fix: Rule of Three
"When brainstorming, limit yourself to the top three points."
Issue 2 - Filler words:
Primary fillers: "like," "you know," "right" (with upward inflection)
"These are the most prevalent." [Shows exact timestamps where they occurred]
Real reaction: "I usually don't have these. I pride myself on speaking decently well presenting-wise. But in a call, you're taken from your zone of comfort into a different zone."
Issue 3 - Fear of silence:
"You often use a filler word to bridge the gap between your thoughts."
Nuance discovered: "I've used pauses before. Then clients will use it to speak over me."
Context matters for feedback interpretation.
Body language breakdown received:
Eye Contact:
"Strong and consistent. Minor natural breaks when thinking at 7:35 are acceptable."
Posture:
"You're leaned back for much of the call. While this can signal relaxation, for high-stakes C-suite conversation, slightly leaning forward non-verbally communicates higher engagement."
Context revealed: "This was me in a hotel in Dominican Republic working from there. I can totally see how I would be more relaxed than normal."
Gestures:
"Your energy is fantastic." (More praise than critique here)
Client body language analysis:
Moments of Conviction:
"Client was most engaged when you provided clear, actionable frameworks. He nods his head affirmatively and leans in slightly when you start to discuss concrete models like RICE."
Moments of Uncertainty:
"Client displayed contemplative or slightly overwhelmed body language when your pace accelerated." [Exact timestamps provided]
Speech mastery feedback:
Pacing issue:
"Many of your declarative statements end with an upward inflection. Like: 'And that's a huge opportunity, right?'"
Why this happens: "Sometimes I do that to make sure the person on the other end of the line is still with me."
AI catches the pattern you rationalize away.
Talk-to-listen ratio:
"Ratio is heavily skewed towards you talking."
Context provided: "This client wanted to listen more than they wanted to share with me."
Lesson: "Pull out some nuance. Keep in mind all your cases will be different."
Why AI feedback works better than human feedback:
"If anyone ever told me this themselves, I would definitely go into defensive mode. But because AI is neutral, I will take it more without the grain of salt."
Human feedback = ego protection kicks in
AI feedback = pure data you can act on
The systematic improvement loop:
1. Record every call (with permission)
2. Run Arya analysis within 24 hours
3. Note top 3 improvements
4. Practice those 3 things on next call
5. Re-analyze next call
6. Track improvement over time
Speaking headlines principle:
Instead of: Five complex concepts in 12 seconds
Do: Three clear points with pauses between
Instead of: 45 minutes of you talking
Do: 20 minutes talking, 25 minutes listening, 15 minutes co-creating
Instead of: Leaning back entire call
Do: Lean slightly forward during key moments
The compounding effect:
Week 1: Identify you use "like" 47 times per call
Week 2: Down to 23 times
Week 4: Down to 5 times
Week 8: Clients describe you as "articulate and confident"
Small fixes compound into massive presence upgrades.
Critical reminder:
"A lot of times you will get a client that pushes your boundaries of how well composed you can be. Other times, it's on you. You've had an off day, busy day, might not have given 100% to someone that paid for your time."
The Arya system catches both: When it's the client being difficult, and when you're off your game.
