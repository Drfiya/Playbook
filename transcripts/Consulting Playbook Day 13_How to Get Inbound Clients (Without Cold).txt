Strategy 1 - Reddit (The Underrated Goldmine):
The logic: If language models are primarily trained using Reddit as information for how humans behave, then why not go to where they live and where people are complaining about what they're struggling with?
Your avatar of whoever you're trying to sell to or consult for is complaining about their problem somewhere.
Real case study:

How: Joined r/VoiceAgents and r/AISolutions forums, answered questions without selling, just solved problems.
Result: DMs full from Reddit where everyone has fake usernames.

The principle: The more you are helpful to other people, the more you're recognized, the more people are familiar with your name, the more likely they are willing to reach out proactively.

Strategy 3 - LinkedIn (Without The AI Slop):
The problem: People use and abuse LinkedIn the wrong way. I hate the way people use LinkedIn. It's very parasitic. The AI slop is very apparent.
The telltale signs:
- Rocket emojis üöÄ

Why dilute your brand, dilute your offering by having these little sloppy lines that don't make you look as white glove and polished as you should?
The correct LinkedIn strategy:
Step 1: Answer questions on viral posts
Go in on viral posts where people are asking questions, where the author doesn't have bandwidth to answer said question. Answer the questions as detailed as possible.
The arbitrage: Every single time someone likes your comment, it shows up on their connections feeds. The more genuine sauce you add...
What NOT to do: Don't take the whole thing of the thread and post and say can you please go answer that question in a very thoughtful way and write like a human or else I will send you to Mars.
What to do: Answer it yourself, make it look superhuman (lowercase). If you refuse to type it out or dictate it the way I dictate most responses in community, then at least make it look very human.
Step 2: Recycle your answers
You'll be able to recycle a lot of your answers to questions between Reddit, between Skool, between LinkedIn. People have same problems, just venting about it on different platforms and through different modalities.
Step 3: Build connections and offer free webinars
After providing series of sauce:

- Offer useful n8n workflows or things that actually work
The LinkedIn template trap:
Bad strategy: Comment Donkey Kong and I will send you this horrific template that I downloaded from n8n template library. I have no idea how to set it up.
Why it works short-term: Because people are naive on LinkedIn, they will comment Donkey Kong and you'll get more visibility. You'll see hundreds of comments and likes.
Why it kills you long-term: Those people build a bad reputation. One, they didn't build it themselves, meaning they have no idea how to set it up if people ask questions. Two, if they don't know how to use n8n and act like they do, once they get on discovery call, people can smell when you're full of it.
The right approach: If you actually share things you've built, you always have that tacit knowledge, that been there done that. You'll be surprised how basic of a thing that's actually useful, that actually works, that people will value.
